Every year the best rookie in the National League and the best rookie in the American League receive the Rookie of the Year award. After receiving this award there are expectations, and often those athletes have a sophomore slump. Which is a season that does not live up to their ROY winning season. Here I have looked at the the winners of the Rookie of the Year award from 2002-2012, besides pitchers, and compared their Hits, Runs Batted Ins, and On base position plus slugging. The players that I have labeled have won the Rookie of the Year and stood out in seasons to come. The other grey dots are everybody else in those 11 seasons. The dot way out past 250 hits in a season was Ichiro Suzuki in the 2004 season. Suzuki was the American League Rookie of the Year in 2001. In 2004, he would break Hall of Famer, George Sislerâ€™s 84-year-old record of hits in a single season. The National League Rookie of the Year winner was Albert Pujols who would go on to hit 703 career home runs ranking fourth all-time behind Barry Bonds, Hank Aaron, and Babe Ruth.

In 2007, Geovany Soto won National League Rookie of the Year and Dustin Pedroia won American League Rookie of the Year. The way their careers would turn out after this award would be polar opposites.

Geovany Soto stands out with 86 RBIs in his Rookie of the Year campaign in 2008 with the Chicago Cubs. That year Soto would go on to be an All Star, but that would be the highlight of his career statistically. In 2008 he had 141 hits, but he would never get more than 100 hits in a season again. Geovany Soto is not a household name for a reason. After 2011, he would leave the Cubs and continue to jump from team to team. The sophomore slump was real for Soto.

On the opposite side of things, Dustin Pedrioa would rank lower in RBIs compared to other ROY winners, and slightly higher on Hits. Pedrioa would go on to be a lifelong Red Sox. He would go on to be a four-time All-Star and four-time golden glove second basemen. In 2008, the year after he won the ROY award, he would win American League MVP and lead the league in Runs, Hits, and Doubles. Pedroia would help the Red Sox win two World Series in 2007 and 2013 as well.

In 2012, Bryce Harper and Mike Trout would win the Rookie of the Year awards. Harper and Trout has continued to stay in the headlines on their respective teams in the last 11 seasons.

Mike Trout is the face of baseball. Trout is a 10-time All Star, three-time American League MVP (four times finishing second in MVP race), and nine-time silver slugger. In this time, the Angels have sucked. The Halos made the post season once in Troutâ€™s era in 2014, and they lost in the first round. Trout is undeniably one of the best athletes to have ever played the game.

At the same time, Bryce Harper has made a completely different name for himself in baseball. Harper was known as a hot head early in his career making him a player that fans loved to hate. Harper is a seven-time All Star, two-time National League MVP, and two-time silver slugger. Since Harper hasnâ€™t played for such a painfully awful team like the Angels, made it to the post season four times with the Nationals and once with the Phillies. While the Nationals never made it past the National League Divisional Series, in 2022 the Phillies made it all the way to the World Series. Harper was a big part of this battle. The Phillies were not suppose to be in the World Series, as they clinched the last playoff spot in the National League. But Harper would go on to win the National League Championship Series MVP, as he hit six home runs, batted .347 and had 13 RBIs. Harper has a postseason career batting average of .273. Therefor, Harper has made more of an impact when it comes to team success.

This whole time we have been focusing on offensive stats, and we have failed to mention one of the most important positions on the field. The pitchers who has won Rookie of the Year between 2002 and 2012. The names that stand out the most are Justin Verlander and Craig Kimbrel.

You may know Craig Kimbrel as the relief pitcher that hangs his arm out all wonky before he pitches the ball. Kimbrel won the National League Rookie of the Year in 2011 as he led the league in saves and games finished. Kimbrel has played in eight post seasons, and he won the World Series in 2018 with the Boston Red Sox. The eight-time All Star has been a key part to many championship caliber teams.

On the other hand, Justin Verlander has been one of the most dominant starting pitchers in the last 17 seasons. Verlander is a three-time Cy Young winner, nine-time All Star, 2011 American League MVP, and 2006 American League Rookie of the Year. Verlander has also led the league in strikeouts in five different seasons. After not pitching at all in the 2021 season due to having Tommy John Surgery, Verlander won the CY Young and led the Astros to a World Series victory in 2022.

Here I am comparing Verlander to other Cy Young winners of the past couple of years in the AL and NL. Many of the pitchers he is compared to has also won several Cy Young awards, and have been a part of many World Series Championship teams.

Winning Rookie of the Year may be more important for a position player than a pitcher. Position players seem to have better seasons in their youth, as pitchers seem to improve with age. Out of the the 2002-2012 ROY winners, Harper, Trout, Verlander, and Pedrioa have made the most out of their careers since receiving the award.
